# Proof reading

Improve the writing without rewriting it. Preserve the author's voice.

1. Read the whole piece first to grasp the intent, audience, and tone.
2. Fix the mechanics: grammar, spelling, punctuation, and typos.
3. Improve clarity: tighten wordy or convoluted sentences, cut redundancy, and resolve vague phrasing — but keep the meaning identical.
4. Enforce consistency across the piece: terminology, capitalization, tense, point of view, voice, and formatting.
5. Smooth the flow: transitions between sentences and paragraphs, logical ordering, no abrupt jumps.
6. Flag, do not silently change: sentences that are ambiguous, factually unsupported, or where you cannot tell what the author meant. Note them for the author to decide.
7. Return the edited text, plus a short list of the substantive changes and any flags.

Rules:
- Make surgical edits. Do not rewrite wholesale or impose your own style over theirs.
- Preserve the author's voice, register, and intent — you are editing, not authoring.
- When a fix would change meaning, flag it instead of applying it.
